Homemade Pizza

Homemade pizza turns simple ingredients into a delicious meal. The dough, made from flour, water, yeast, salt, and olive oil, is topped with tomato sauce and your favorite toppings. Baking at a high temperature results in a crisp, golden crust and perfectly melted toppings.

Making pizza at home is easy and customizable. You can create anything from a classic Margherita to a unique combination of toppings, resulting in a fresh, tasty pizza that's better than store-bought.

Ingredients

  • 500g all-purpose flour
  • 325ml warm water
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 7g active dry yeast (1 packet)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 can (400g) crushed tomatoes
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tsp sugar (optional)
  • Mozzarella cheese
  • Add your favorite toppings (pepperoni, mushrooms, bell peppers, etc.)

Steps

    • Mix warm water, sugar, and yeast. Let sit for 5 minutes until frothy.
    • Combine flour and salt in a large bowl. Add yeast mixture and olive oil. Mix until dough forms.
    • Knead dough on a floured surface for 10 minutes until smooth.
    • Place dough in an oiled bowl, cover, and let rise for 1-2 hours until doubled in size.
    • Heat olive oil in a saucepan. Add minced garlic and cook for 1 minute.
    • Add crushed tomatoes, oregano, basil, salt, pepper, and sugar. Simmer for 15 minutes.
    • Preheat oven to highest setting (around 250°C / 482°F). If using a pizza stone, place it in the oven.
    • Divide dough into 2-3 pieces. Roll each piece into a circle on a floured surface.
    • Transfer dough to parchment paper. Spread sauce, add cheese, and your favorite toppings.
    • Bake for 10-15 minutes until crust is golden and cheese is bubbly.
    • Let cool slightly, then slice and enjoy!
